Overview

We also seeked feedback about our wireframe during this process from both the class and David, a Senior Product Designer at Warp, in order to improve.

Design Decisions

We wanted to have both a team page and personal page in order to seperate the drive content, but we felt that having a left bar would be beneficial to put glancable information. Based on the project brief, our order of priority was: information about my files, information about my team, learning more about Warp, managing my account, and view usage metrics. Therefore, the drive portion took up a majority of the screen, but the left bar also included information about the activity, current live sessions and Warp resources.

Inspiration From Sketches

We noticed that many of the sketches explored placing content at both the center and sides of the screens. Quite a few of us had left side bars with information and the items about files were placed at the center of the screen, so we kept that in the wireframe. In addition, since people showed flows navigating to a team screen we thought making them both accessible from the home page would be easier. We also liked the idea of having users be able to toggle the information on the left and even hide it if they did not want to constant see it.

Feedback on Wireframes

Here is our five minute Loom video that we created for David!

Below we have included some of the feedback that we recieved on the first iteration wireframe where red is for David (Warp Product Designer), blue is for Vanessa (Professor) and green is for the class.

  • If a user is on multiple teams than a UI may not be the best design choice.
  • Unclear how to get from current view into terminal and how to get from terminal to this view.
  • Where does black top arrow on left go back to?
  • People like that the design/model feels similar to Google Drive so people will know how to operate it.
  • Ensure that the order of modules on the left is based on what is most important to the user.
  • Having too many recents may defeat point of an aggregator. In addition, it may be unnecessary to have a side scroll for recents since they are not typically interacted with as much.
  • Show the flow when you open a folder.
  • People may want more control over how they see the items in their drive. For instance, you could have an option to toggle between list and gallery mode, filters and sorting.
